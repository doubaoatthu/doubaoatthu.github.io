Keras: How to Combine RNNs and CNNs

Posted on August 29, 2016 | keras, deep learning, neural networks, cnn, rnn

In keras, cnn layers comsume input tensors as a whole, while rnn layers consume tensors as sequences.

For example, if we have a tensor of dimension m * n, cnn will consume it as the tensor itself. While rnn will consume it as a sequence, that is, m vectors, each has the length of n.

We can use a buildin wrapper called TimeDistributed to connect cnn and rnn.

tick_input = Input(shape = (tick_timesteps, 1, cnndim1, cnndim2, cnndim3))
model = Sequential()
model.add(Convolution3D(32, 5,5,5, border_mode = 'valid',input_shape=(1, cnndim1, cnndim2, cnndim3), dim_ordering='th'))
model.add(Flatten())
processed = TimeDistributed(model)(tick_input)
lstmout = LSTM(60, return_sequences=True)(processed)
